Migrating sketch to figma

At the beginning of the pandemic, Q4 like many businesses had transitioned to a fully remote workforce. With a well-established investor relations product suite and design system to match, the need for process change seemed unnecessary.

However, within weeks of the transition, a major bottleneck in the design process was the Sketch and Abstract workflow and design library. Requiring constant communication, file updates, and connections, Sketch and Abstract became cumbersome to work with.

Company

Q4 Inc.

Date

July 2020 – August 2020

Duration

1 month

Roles

Lead | Product Manager

Problem

With a recently dispersed team, usage of Q4’s Sketch and Abstract workflow and design system had constrained design output.

Solution

Transition the full organization’s design documentation and design system to Figma.

Planning & Execution

Drafting a pitch

With an org-wide transition, there was going to be a large part of the company impacted. Identifying stakeholders beyond leadership pushed the need to build relationships with team leads and senior IC staff, as they would be the most affected. With those team members bought in, the pitch was broken down into:

  • Problems: What’s the need for the change? What issues were we running into with the process, as well as the design system?
  • Tool breakdown: What alternatives exist that solve the aforementioned problems (at this time, Figma was clearly the best option)
  • Tool breakdown: What alternatives exist that solve the aforementioned problems (at this time, Figma was the best option)
  • Cost changes: For the cost management team, how will the transition impact the budget?
  • Roadmap: How are we going to make this transition (short, medium, long term)? How do we know this change has solved the issues?

The transition

Working with development on the design system first to simplify the transition, my efforts focused on electrons, moving upward to atoms as progress was made. This also included defining a process for design system maintenance, and migrating to (at the time) new features such as variants and auto layout.

Post the design system completion, the next road-mapped step was a dedicated week of design effort. This helped delegate work to the broader team, ensuring the fastest time possible in transitioning, especially as we began a net new product initiative.

Tips

Power week

  • Having exact goals and plan for each day ensured no friction or delays
  • Daily progress check-ins, and a end of week summary for broader teams allowed for collaboration if they had capacity.
  • Both for the design and development team, hosting a series of introductory workshops set the team up for success in execution

Transitioning pages

  • The design system as a referencable resource in many cases can instantly connect to files, getting it done first avoids a lot of headaches.
  • Some components will be broken in the transition, vetting files after transition rather than later avoids further future delays.
  • Define what a file being “transitioned” means to keep the team aligned.
  • In many ways this is “starting fresh”, consider updates to page structure, team format or org design now before files are moved.

Issues to watch out for

  • Breaking electrons was very common in new files. This meant utilizing “select all > specific colour” as a work around, but that only works for certain colour naming systems.
  • Misalignment of standards can come up regularly, review work at regular intervals compared against the definition of done.
  • Prioritizing a primary versus secondary file early keeps momentum. Files transitioned later in the process can be expected to be lower quality.
  • Identify where variance from the design system can occur, or where it can be revisited when worked on in the future. Complete connections in every file may be completely unnecessary

Results

Data

With some additional coaching required from the design team to development, collaboration became significantly easier. With the transition successful, the team was able to deliver net new conferencing product in 8 months. In total this effort resulted in:

  • 60+ pages transitioned
  • 50+ components migrated and updated
  • Work was completed in 1 total month of effort, 1 week of full design team effort
  • Artifacts created for the one week sprint became the basis of the design system definition

For those new to Figma, I created a introductory guide. It explained product as a whole, the best practices, and the contribution process for the design system which was owned by myself moving forward.

Learnings

On top of the tips listed above, this process taught:

  • Understand your audience: As tempting as it was to dive into Figma features, the most successful part of the entire pitch was the cost.
  • Orient process on speed: No team member would continue the cadence of the design system or transition efforts beyond the one-week effort. This required a revision of the initial process, which organically changed with the team over time.
