Uniting design systems

Through growth, acquisitions, and mergers, Geotab has created a powerful suite of tools that cover the full needs of a fleet. This steady growth had resulted in a near product space monopoly, at the expense of becoming monolithic.

The sheer size and complexity of Geotab had slowed it to a crawl.

Company

Geotab

Date

August 2021 – August 2022

Duration

12 months

Roles

Lead | Project Manager

Problem

Development and design efforts are hindered by the isolated construction of each product, and in many cases, each feature.

Solution

Unify the 6 distinct design systems used by all products into a single source of truth, both in code and in Figma.

Learn

Auditing everything

Reviewing current products and their respective design systems kick-started an understanding of the maturity of both design and design systems at Geotab. Priority of the audit was given to the most popular products: MyGeotab, Drive, and MyAdmin.

Within the design and engineering teams, impromptu workshops and coffee chats painted a clearer picture of design system engagement and established processes.

Findings

Design systems and their respective process was non-existent, and no one took ownership of them. The audit also revealed:

  • Disconnect in design systems to their lives products
  • Assortment of design systems used across the suite
  • Multiple instances of duplicated components
  • An estimated 25% of development effort goes towards recreating previously completed components for the MyGeotab product alone
  • Development release cycles can take up to a year and need to support versions over 5 years old

Establish

Building a process

Volunteering as the owner of the design systems, establishing what working with design systems meant in the organization became a priority. This meant standardizing the requirements and definitions of done for design system contributions. This had to be done in a format that fits individuals' regular process to enable delegation and team-wide (20+ individuals) involvement.

To get development’s involvement early, Jira became the workhorse for tracking and delegating. Figma’s design system metrics became the defacto understanding of improvements and engagement, and eventually utilizing the branch/merge process to improve contribution.

Unifying the design systems - electrons, atoms

With drastically different products and use cases, an open discussion had to happen among product experts in designers and developers on bringing the products together. The tactic was workshops, initially regularly occurring but eventually becoming ad hoc.

Active contribution style resulted in efficient workshops, consolidating group understanding of design systems in their respective products today. This more active workshop moved async after each session as electrons and atoms were consolidated by myself.

Collaborate

Migrating to code

Thanks to the engagement and buy-in of engineering, pitching for development resourceswas succesful. The intention to cut down product complexity while simplifying development resourcing resonated.

Coinciding with the transition of multiple products to react, the design system would serve as the guide for teams to migrate to the new library. Creating touchpoints along with further hand-off processes with the dev team focused on the project, solidified the process moving forward.

Making it public-facing

There is a massive marketplace of in-product solutions completed by third parties. Similar to Geotab, these products are visually quite divergent from the rest of the suite.

Working with a product manager, we scoped a third-party development facing website that would utilize both documentation from a design perspective, as well as implementation documentation from a development perspective.

Results

In data

The alignment of design systems dropped component detachments by almost 70%. Engagement with the primary design system also nearly quadrupled, as we deprecated 3 of the conflicting design systems, leaving 2 others for product-specific components (which were connected to the primary design system).

The developed design system is still in testing but with promising results from all of the opted-in external developers. Documentation has led to extensive discussion and knowledge-sharing, while the finalized NPM package is still being completed.

As the design system is constantly growing, short and long-term roadmaps were created that would follow the development of the design system. Additional metrics and accompanying goals were also constructed to gauge the effectiveness of the design system from a development and design perspective moving forward.

Learnings

  • Establish process early: Orienting the team around a distinct process and standard of work had immediate positive repercussions. It carried beyond design systems, and gave a common language for team members to discuss material.
  • Process not a one size fits all: The first reaction when moving to owning the design system at Geotab was to instate the process I had created at my previous company. It became clear quickly that the scale of teams, pace of work, and impact of the design system required a vastly different process./li>
  • Async and delegation vs sync: Workshopping for knowledge sharing removed exclusive domain knowledge as an issue, but became difficult to synthesize on an individual level. Delegating some of these efforts, as well as synthesizing in-workshop would have sped up the process for design system definition.
