Thesis

Distracted Driving

With the growth in smartphone ownership, the need to use devices has had many consequences, in fact you are now more likely to be killed by a distracted driver than a drunk one.

While manufacturers are integrating the phone into cars to mitigate distractions, these newer vehicles are not accessible to young drivers.

Following a detailed research and testing process, a product was created to mitigate distracted driving by simplifying phone content and changing the method of interaction with it. This finalized product took the form of a heads up display

Company

Independent

Date

September 2017 – April 2018

Project Duration

8 months

My Roles

Product Designer
UX Researcher

Why?

Nearly 80% of all accidents today are from distracted driving, with a quarter being from phone distractions. This means you are now more likely to be killed by a texter than a drunk driver.

While newer cars have more and more technology to help you stay focused, the majority do not have access to a new car. This means that young people who need this technology the most do not have it.

What?

glance is a heads up display, designed to better display your phone content. glance displays the most used information for the user: music, gps, and text information.

Moving the driver’s view to the windshield eliminates any time needed to look away from the road to their phone, and allows for users to still use their peripheral vision.

How?

Utilizing smartphones pre-embedded technology, glance takes information that smartphones supply to users, and simplifies it. This simplification prevents distractions and only requires active engagement when needed.

Using the smartphone as the middle-man, interacting with the content moves from a physical interaction (requiring hand, eyes, and ears) to auditory with minimal visual.

Learning as much as I can

Personal Inspiration

We’ve all heard the daily news cycle “police are cracking down on distracted driving”. Yet somehow, accidents caused by distraction are on the rise. For my capstone project of Interaction Design, I decided to focus on this problem:

How might I combat distracted driving?

Starting with a broad challenge statement acted as a guide for my research to define the problem space.

Secondary Research

To start this process a secondary research round on the facts of the issue was completed. This included finding who is affected by this issue, the cause of the issue, and solutions that have been tested/implemented.

  • You are now twice as likely to be killed by a distracted driver then a drunk one
  • Phones in particular require a larger piece of your brain and concentration to engage with them than other distractions
  • The most common people to be distracted are new/young drivers
  • These are also the most likely to be killed in an accident
  • Car companies are now actively working towards mitigating distracted driving, as are multiple app and phone manufacturers

Primary Research

Moving to primary research to refine my understanding and offer more clear direction for the project, I conducted 8 in depth interviews and accumulated 62 unique survey responses. From these I pulled out multiple insights including:

  • From ex-police officer, distracted driving was not a focus in curriculum
  • People do not fear getting caught/getting in an accident while distracted
  • The most common distraction is passengers, but the most distracting are peoples phones
  • People get more distracted the more confident they get
  • Phone users feel the need to keep checking it, and would not be willing to have it constrained whole driving

Refine Challenge Statement

At this time some I refined my initial very broad challenge statement. Based on my research findings of teenagers being most affected and phones identified as the biggest distraction, my new challenge became

How might I better integrate phones into cars that millennials drive the most?

Focusing research

Persona and Interviews

Focusing on the young drivers most affected by this and most likely to get in accidents a persona was crafted. This persona guided 5 interviews and an additional 18 survey responses.

I ended up with quite a few more key insights including:

  • The majority of drivers don’t have access to any new car technology, as they typically can’t afford it
  • Over 50% of users interviews and surveyed admitted to using their phone while driving
  • Interviewed users had little interest in phone limiting technology
  • Interviewed members felt the need still to check even if there is no messages (ghost notifications was a common issue)
  • Users typically only used a few key functions on there phone while driving when they would (GPS, Music, and Messaging)

With a grasp on the user, I mapped their current journey and their pain-points that my solution could solve.

Rapid ideation and prototyping

Brainstorming and testing

I moved directly into ideating from research. In total, around 50 unique ideas were generated, which were then refined down to four based on feasibility, novelty, and relevance.

For the 4 concepts I narrowed down too, I wireframed rough concepts and then brought them to potential users of the solution. This immediately raises any shortcomings of one ian idea. Some new insights came from this including:

  • The need to find positives in people's driving, not negatives
  • Modularity is important to users
  • Touchscreens have no feedback tactile feedback, require full focus
  • The final solution should not be a distraction in itself, or at the very least limit the interaction to an easier method then current
  • Any phone content shown, should be summarized and simplified
  • Users need alternative methods for interacting with phone

At this point it was clear the heads up display solution resonated best with the audience, and fit the defined pain-points of the users best.

Competitor Analysis

Before moving to a refined concept, I spent more time getting a grasp on other companies' products in this space. I found a few common issues from user reviews. Some of those were:

  • The HUD’s that use your phone to sit inside it are frustrating to setup and use
  • Digital screens make the product very expensive

Refined HUD UI design

From my research quite a few design requirements arose that I kept in mind in my iterations. These included:

  • Information must be simple
  • Cut out anything unnecessary
  • Colours need to contrast the environment
  • Fonts need to be legible while driving
  • Animations need to be non-flashy, simple
  • Prioritize information by users choice
  • Content stays in place otherwise

To adhere to these, the functionality was descoped to the 3 key identified phone uses while driving. These were music, GPS, and messaging. For these functionalities I iterated multiple renditions to find the best possible layout.

As touch screen interactions are extremely attention grabbing, using the phones voice commands for interactions was the best method, so I designed with that intention in mind.

The final iteration strips back all unnecessary cognitive load and gives users the essentials to be able to make decisions in each core functionality with each screen dedicated to one focus.

3D Product Design

Following a similar theme as the UI design, simplicity was the focus for 3D product The goal was to keep the product affordable, while still creating a beautifully simple shape. The end item accomplishes both.

How things ended up

Product Testing

As I did not have access to the likes of eye trackers or virtual environment testing, I had to design an impromptu usability test to determine the products efficacy.

To test the product, I 3D printed a prototype and used a controlled phone screen to play an animated version of my designed UI. This clip of my UI was then played remotely when users interacted with the device.

To complete this test, I had 2 drivers drive the same vehicle in a controlled area, and then got them to perform a series of tasks involving their phone. To measure success I timed them, noted issues, any behaviours, and followed up with questions on their experience.

Outcomes

Test Results

I found from the tests conducted that the product succeeded in lowering mishap rates and time with eyes of road. Users accomplished tasks in a similar time frame to using their phone, but spent significantly less time with their eyes off the road.

The users remarked that they because still had their peripheral vision available to them they were more comfortable completing the tasks

To bring this project further, I would further develop the prototype product,refining the 3D design, display, and UI. Then conduct testing with a larger group to understand what further improvements can be made.
